To produce a full institutional-quality investment pitch for Micron Technology (MU), including thesis, valuation, and risk analysis.

## Out-of-Sample Results
This project focuses on providing a comprehensive investment thesis and valuation based on publicly available data as of April 2026. The models are designed for forward-looking analysis rather than in-sample performance.

## Limitations and Potential Improvements
- **Data Granularity**: Reliance on publicly available summary data may limit the depth of analysis compared to proprietary institutional data.
- **Model Complexity**: The DCF model is simplified for illustrative purposes. A more robust model would incorporate detailed operating expenses, working capital changes, and sensitivity analysis.
- **Market Volatility**: The semiconductor industry is highly cyclical. Future market conditions could significantly impact projections.
- **Qualitative Factors**: While considered, qualitative factors like management quality and competitive moats are difficult to quantify and are reflected primarily in growth and margin assumptions.

## Lessons Learned
- The importance of robust data sourcing from primary documents (SEC filings).
- The iterative nature of financial modeling and the need for constant assumption refinement.
- The challenge of balancing conciseness with comprehensive detail in an investment pitch.
