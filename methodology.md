# Investment Pitch Methodology

This document outlines the methodology and assumptions used to develop the institutional investment pitch for Micron Technology (MU).

## 1. Company Selection
Micron Technology (MU) was selected due to its prominent position in the memory semiconductor market, particularly its leadership in High Bandwidth Memory (HBM) which is critical for Artificial Intelligence (AI) workloads. The company's recent financial performance and strategic initiatives in the AI sector present a compelling investment case.

## 2. Data Gathering
Financial data, SEC filings (10-K, 10-Q), and investor presentations were primarily sourced from:
- **Micron Investor Relations**: [https://investors.micron.com/](https://investors.micron.com/)
- **SEC EDGAR**: [https://www.sec.gov/edgar/](https://www.sec.gov/edgar/)
- **Yahoo Finance (yfinance)**: For historical stock prices and general market data.

## 3. Fundamental Analysis

### a. Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) Analysis
**Objective**: To estimate the intrinsic value of Micron's equity based on its projected future free cash flows.

**Key Assumptions**:
- **Revenue Growth**: Projections are based on market trends in the memory sector, particularly the AI-driven demand for HBM and DDR5. A super-cycle peak is assumed in 2026, followed by a normalization of growth rates.
- **EBITDA Margins**: Reflect the expected pricing power and cost efficiencies driven by HBM3E and advanced manufacturing nodes. Higher margins are projected during the peak of the super-cycle.
- **Capital Expenditures (CAPEX)**: High CAPEX is assumed due to the capital-intensive nature of semiconductor manufacturing and Micron's investments in new technologies and capacity expansion.
- **Depreciation & Amortization (D&A)**: Estimated as a percentage of CAPEX, reflecting the asset base and investment cycle.
- **Tax Rate**: A statutory tax rate of 15% is applied.
- **Working Capital**: Assumed to be neutral for simplicity in this model, implying that changes in current assets and liabilities offset each other.
- **Weighted Average Cost of Capital (WACC)**: Estimated at 10%, reflecting Micron's cost of equity and debt, and its business risk.
- **Terminal Growth Rate**: A conservative long-term growth rate of 3% is used, reflecting the mature phase of the industry cycle.

**Methodology**:
1.  Projected revenues, EBITDA, D&A, and CAPEX for a 5-year explicit forecast period.
2.  Calculated Free Cash Flow to Firm (FCFF) for each year.
3.  Determined a Terminal Value using the Gordon Growth Model.
4.  Discounted all future cash flows (FCFF and Terminal Value) back to the present using the WACC to arrive at Enterprise Value.
5.  Adjusted Enterprise Value for net debt (total debt minus cash and equivalents) to derive Equity Value.
6.  Divided Equity Value by shares outstanding to get the intrinsic price per share.

### b. Comparable Company Analysis (Comps)
**Objective**: To determine Micron's relative valuation compared to its peers in the semiconductor industry.

**Selected Peers**:
- **SK Hynix**: A direct competitor in the memory market, particularly in HBM.
- **Samsung Electronics**: A diversified technology conglomerate with a significant memory business.
- **NVIDIA**: Included as a proxy for AI-driven growth, given its integral role in the AI ecosystem and its impact on HBM demand.

**Key Multiples**:
- **Enterprise Value to EBITDA (EV/EBITDA)**: A common metric for capital-intensive industries, normalizing for differences in capital structure and tax rates.
- **Price to Earnings (P/E)**: A widely used equity valuation multiple.

**Methodology**:
1.  Gathered current and projected financial metrics (EBITDA, EPS) for Micron and its selected peers.
2.  Calculated relevant valuation multiples for each peer.
3.  Applied median peer multiples to Micron's projected financials to derive implied valuation ranges.

## 4. Catalyst Identification and Timeline
Catalysts were identified through a review of company announcements, industry reports, and market news. These include product launches (HBM3E ramp), financial guidance, and strategic partnerships.

## 5. Risk Factors and Bear Case Analysis
Potential risks were assessed, including industry cyclicality, execution risks related to new technology adoption, and geopolitical factors impacting global supply chains. A bear case scenario was considered to evaluate potential downside.

## 6. Position Sizing and Portfolio Fit Strategy
Recommendations for position sizing were based on the risk/reward profile of the investment and its potential fit within a diversified institutional portfolio.

## 7. Pitch Deck Creation
The 15-page pitch deck was structured to present a clear, concise, and compelling investment narrative, adhering to institutional quality standards. Visualizations and key data points were integrated to support the thesis.
